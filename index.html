Drift Privacy Policy

Last updated: 21 June 2026 · Terms & Conditions

This Privacy Policy explains what information Drift (“the App,” “we,” “us”) collects, why, and what choices you have. Drift is designed around a simple principle: collect as little as possible, and let messages disappear by design.

1. Who we are

Drift is operated by Michael Duggan. If you have questions about this policy or how your data is handled, you can contact us at: michaelduggan112358@gmail.com.

2. What information we collect

2.1 Account information

To use Drift, you choose a username and a PIN. We do not require or collect your name, email address, or phone number. Your PIN is never stored in plain text — it is hashed with a unique random salt before being saved, so we cannot read or recover your actual PIN.

2.2 Messages

Messages you send are stored only for as long as the sender’s chosen retention period (the default is 7 days, adjustable per message down to as little as 1 minute, or up to 7 days). After this period, messages are automatically and permanently deleted from our systems.

2.3 Contacts

If you choose to save another user as a contact, we store that association so Drift can show it to you. You can remove a contact at any time.

2.4 Technical and infrastructure data

Drift is built on Google Firebase. As with most cloud infrastructure, Google may process technical metadata (such as IP addresses) as part of operating its servers and delivering the service securely. This processing is governed by Google’s role as our infrastructure provider (sub-processor), and is not used by us for tracking, advertising, or profiling.

2.5 Push notifications

If you enable notifications, your device is assigned a push token by Google Firebase Cloud Messaging, which we store so the App can notify you of new messages. This token does not identify you personally and is only used to deliver notifications to your device. You can disable notifications at any time in Settings.

3. What we do not collect

  • We do not collect your real name, email address, or phone number.
  • We do not request access to your contacts, camera, microphone, or photos.
  • We do not use advertising trackers or sell personal data to third parties.
  • We do not retain messages beyond the deletion timer you or your conversation partner selected.

4. Why we process your data (legal basis)

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract: processing your username, PIN, messages, and contacts is necessary to provide the core messaging service you have requested.
  • Legitimate interests: limited technical processing (e.g. by our infrastructure provider) necessary to keep the service secure and operational.

5. How long we keep your data

Messages are deleted automatically according to the timer set at the time of sending. Account information (username and hashed PIN) is retained until you delete your account. Upon account deletion, your account record is marked for deletion; associated messages and contacts are removed as that functionality is implemented.

6. Your rights under GDPR

If you are located in the European Economic Area, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Request deletion of your account and associated data (“right to be forgotten”).
  • Object to or restrict certain processing.
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

You can delete your account directly within the App, under Settings. For other requests, contact us at michaelduggan112358@gmail.com.

7. Data sharing

We do not sell or rent your personal data. Limited data is processed by Google Firebase, which acts as our hosting and database provider (sub-processor) under its own data processing terms. We do not share your data with advertisers or analytics companies.

8. International data transfers

Data may be processed on servers located in the European Economic Area or other regions where our infrastructure provider operates data centres. Where data is transferred outside the EEA, appropriate safeguards (such as Standard Contractual Clauses) are relied upon by our infrastructure provider.

9. Children’s privacy

Drift is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under this age. If you believe a child has created an account, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the “Last updated” date above. Continued use of the App after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

11. Contact us

For any questions about this Privacy Policy or your data, contact: michaelduggan112358@gmail.com.
